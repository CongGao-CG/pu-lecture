CENTER FOR CAREER DEVELOPMENT


The Center for Career Development assists undergraduate and graduate students in preparing for careers that resonate with their skills, strengths, interests, and values. Take advantage of recruiting events, sponsored programs, and skill-building workshops, and meet one-on-one with a career adviser (in person or over Zoom) to discuss your interests and explore career options in academia, industry, social impact, entrepreneurship, and other fields.

Schedule a career advising appointment in Handshake:

Undergraduates: David Belson, Associate Director, Career Advising (STEM specialist) and Ryan Herbert, Assistant Director, Career Advising (STEM specialist)

Graduate Students: Pam Krieger, Ed.D., Senior Associate Director, Graduate Student Career Development (STEM specialist) and Yoonha Shin, Ph.D., Associate Director, Graduate Student Career Development (Generalist)


CENTER FOR STATISTICS AND MACHINE LEARNING


The Center for Statistics and Machine Learning is a campus focal point for data science education and research. To complement Princetonâ€™s degree-conferring programs, the center offers a certificate for graduate students and a minor for undergraduates, in addition to a suite of educational and research initiatives. Enrolled students engage with the basic principles of data, statistics, and machine learning, which is the underlying technology of modern AI. Visit the center online to learn more.


CENTER ON SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY


The Center on Science and Technology (CST) is comprised of dedicated faculty and staff who work to advance STEM literacy across Princeton University and beyond through innovative, interdisciplinary courses and programming, funding for students and faculty, and robust educational research. The CST also oversees the StudioLab, a 2,500-square-foot creative technology space that promotes community and allows everyone, regardless of their prior experience, to create, design, and play.

The Counseling and Psychological Services (CPS) Manager of Outreach, Sue Kim, LCSW, will be holding weekly confidential one-on-one drop-in hours. Sue will be providing in-person sessions (outside of drop-in hours) in room H110 of the EQuad. To schedule a session outside of drop-in hours, email Sue at sk38@princeton.edu. For group counseling support and services offered by CPS please visit the CPS website. CPS also offers urgent care appointments during office hours 9am-4:30pm at the new Frist Health Center at 10 Guyot Lane and the CPS Cares Line which connects you to a telephone counselor is available 24/7(609-258-3141).


GRADUATE STUDENT SERVICES


The Office of the Dean of the Graduate School developed GradFUTURES, a campus-wide professional development program, to empower graduate students with professional skills, competencies, connections and experiences to thrive in graduate school and chart their future with clarity and confidence. Toward those goals, Assistant Dean Sonali Majumdar liaises with graduate students in engineering to discuss individual professional development needs, as well as collaborate on university-wide and department-specific professional development initiatives. Options for meeting with Sonali include:

  • One-on-one office hour meetings to support individual professional development: in-person on Wednesdays; virtual on Fridays

  • Discuss ideas for bespoke departmental professional development events and workshops: schedule in-person or online collaboration meetings

Graduate students can also connect with 2025-26 GradFUTURES Professional Development Associate Godwin Obi (GS, MAE), representing the engineering division, to discuss and communicate ideas for tailored professional development events and workshops.

PICSciE


The Princeton Institute for Computational Science offers a formal and credentialed Graduate Certificate in Computational Science and Engineering. The graduate certificate in computational science and engineering is only open to Princeton University graduate students who are currently enrolled. It is designed to recognize the achievements of students who have undertaken comprehensive training in these topics, both through formal coursework and through research in their subject area. Registration is open throughout the academic year.

PICSciE will host academic seminars and symposia in the coming months. All upcoming events will be posted in the fall.
