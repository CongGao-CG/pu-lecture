Title: Cross-Sectional Learning and Inference for the Stochastic Discount Factor

Abstract: We develop a statistical learning framework for constructing the stochastic discount factor (SDF) portfolio. To address the dimensionality challenge, we extend the MAXSER method (Ao, Li, and Zheng, 2019, RFS) to allow for N >> T; prove that it surely screens for useful characteristics; and establish asymptotic normality for the SDF loading estimates. Using 153 characteristics returns from cross-sectional regressions, our framework not only constructs an SDF with a high out-of-sample Sharpe ratio that successfully prices the cross-section of expected returns, but also allows us to identify key characteristic themes and test the significance of their contributions. Based on joint work with Zhanhui Chen, Yi Ding, and Yingying Li.Short BioXinghua Zheng is a Professor of Business Statistics at HKUST. He received his PhD in statistics from the University of Chicago in 2008. His current research centers on developing statistical theory and methods with a particular focus on financial applications. His research has been published in Annals of Statistics, Annals of Probability, Econometrica, Review of Financial Studies, Journal of Financial Economics, and Journal of Econometrics. He is an elected Fellow of the Society of Financial Econometrics (SoFiE).
